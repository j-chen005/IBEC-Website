§ 01 — Timeline

How it works.

The process is designed to surface genuine intellectual interest — not finance jargon fluency. You don't need to know what a DCF is to start.

Sep 2 — Sep 14

Applications open.

A short written application — three questions, no resume required. We read for how you think, not where you've interned.

Sep 16 — Sep 20

First rounds.

30-minute conversational interview with a committee lead. We'll discuss a recent market event and ask you to share a view.

Sep 22 — Sep 26

Case round.

A 48-hour take-home case. One page, one macro thesis, one conclusion. No model required — just a well-reasoned argument.

Sep 30

Decisions out.

Offers sent by end of day. Apprenticeship track begins the following week with a committee assignment and onboarding session.

§ 02 — What we look for

Three things.

We admit about 15–20 apprentices per semester across all six regional committees. The selection criteria are intentionally straightforward.

01

Intellectual curiosity.

You read things you're not required to read. You form views about why things happen and you can articulate them. You're interested in how the world's economies actually work — not just what's testable.

02

Intellectual honesty.

You can distinguish between what you know and what you think. You update your views when evidence changes. You're not attached to being right — you're attached to being accurate.

03

Willingness to commit.

The apprenticeship is a semester-long commitment. Weekly meetings, a watchlist to maintain, and at least one written pitch. We're not looking for people who will half-attend.

§ 03 — FAQ

Common questions.

If your question isn't here, email us at whartonibec@gmail.com and we'll get back to you within 48 hours.

Do I need a finance background to apply? +

No. We have members from all schools and concentrations. What matters is that you're curious about global markets — not that you've already taken a finance course. The Foundations Program is designed for people who are starting from scratch.

What is the Foundations Program? +

A six-week curriculum that runs in parallel with the apprenticeship for members who are new to financial markets. Topics include: how to read economic data, equity valuation basics, how to read a macro memo, and how WIBEC's investment process works. Optional but strongly recommended for first-semester members.

How much time does the apprenticeship require? +

Plan for 4–6 hours per week. This includes the Monday roundtable, the Thursday investment committee, your committee's weekly meeting, and time spent on your watchlist and pitch research. It scales up during the final two weeks of the semester when apprentice pitches are due.

Can I choose my committee? +

You can indicate a preference in your application and we'll make every effort to match it, but we reserve the right to assign based on committee size and balance. After the first semester, members are free to rotate to another committee if a spot is open.

Is WIBEC a Wharton-only club? +

No. WIBEC is open to all Penn undergraduates. Roughly 40% of current members are from the College, Engineering, or Nursing. The "Wharton" in the name refers to our primary affiliation and where we hold our meetings — not a prerequisite.
